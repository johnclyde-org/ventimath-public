## About the Competition

The William Lowell Putnam Mathematical Competition is an annual mathematics competition for undergraduate college students in the United States and Canada. It is widely considered one of the most prestigious university-level mathematics competitions in the world.

- **Format**: 12 problems (A1-A6, B1-B6) over two 3-hour sessions
- **Scoring**: Each problem is worth 10 points (120 points maximum)
- **Awards**: Putnam Fellows (top 5 individuals), team rankings, honorable mentions
